Who are we?

Helensburgh Friends of Guiding are parents, friends, supporters and members who support the ideals and aspirations if guiding and who actively work to help the units meeting in the Guide Halls in John Street, Helensburgh.

There is a committee consisting of Margaret Morrison (chair), Alan Summers (treasurer), Mary Allen (letting secretary) and Vivien Macpherson (secretary). The Guides and Divison Commissioner meet up with the officials about four times a year to discuss any problems. There should also be a representative poarent from each unit helping us.

This committee keeps the Guide and Brownie halls in good condition and Margaret Morrison spends many hours cleaning and carrying out repairs as needed to ensure the halls are fit for the girls to use.

The units pay a small levy for each girl and we also receive rent from the people/organisations who use the halls. This money is used to pay the electricity, repairs and insurance.

We intend letting you know what is happening in future through FaceBook pages and we hope you might also offer your services to give your daughters the best surroundings for their meetings.

If any parent is able to help us weith repairs, decorating, public relations, fundraising, etc, we would be very grateful to hear from them.

Our email address is info@hfog.org.uk

Centenary Celebrations
Helensburgh Friends of Guiding

Helensburgh Friends of Guiding celebrated its Centenary in 2019.

To mark the occasion we sponsored 63 Brownies, Guides, Rangers, Leaders and Trefoil to a Weekend at Netherurd, and 25 Rainbows plus Leaders to a Sleepover at Catterburn.

Netherurd, in the ScottishBorders, is the Scottish Guiding’s home from home Adventure Centre. It is an Outdoor Centre open to Girlguiding and outside users eg. Scouts, Schools, Churches. All who took part enjoyed a wonderful time. They participated in Night walks, Geocaching, Zip-wiring, Archery, and Campfire. They also found time to build a bear, a crown, cakes and party.

Guide Centenary Celebrations
Saturday, June 14

Brownies from Helensburgh, Arrochar, Rhu, Rosneath, Cove, and Kilcreggan attended the traditional party – lasting 100 minutes – on Saturday, June 14.

Girlguiding members aged seven to ten were joined at the Cove Burgh Hall by ‘VIP visitors’ Eileen Martin, deputy Scottish chief commissioner, and Maxine Gow, county commissioner (Dunbartonshire).
